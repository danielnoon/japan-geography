Japanese Geography and Culture

Daniel Noon

The Islands　島

Hokkaido 北海道

  • “North Sea Way”
  • Northernmost island
  • 10% of land is national parks
  • Cold, snowy winters

Honshu 本州

  • 7th largest island in the world
  • “Main State”
  • Home of the world's largest metropolitan area—Tokyo
  • Warm summers and cool winters

Kyushu 九州

  • “Nine Provinces”
  • Southernmost island
  • Subtropic climate
  • Hot summers

Shikoku 四国

  • “Fourth Country”
  • Southwest of Honshu
  • Smallest and least populous island

Geography and Climate　地理と気候

Physical Geography

  • Mountainous, many dialects
  • No one is farther than 70 miles from the sea, fishing
  • Flat plains
    • Kanto
    • Rice farming

Climate Variety

  • Hokkaido
    • North
    • Cold winters
    • Hemiboreal
    • Snow
      • Sapporo
  • Shikoku and Kyushu
    • South
    • Warm beaches
    • Subtropic
    • Hot, humid summers
  • Tokyo
    • Summers similar to Omaha
    • Ocean moderation

Natural Disasters

  • In the Ring of Fire
  • Earthquakes common
  • Tsunami　津波
  • Typhoons　台風
  • Disasters very common, which builds resiliency

Volcanism

  • Hot springs and onsen
  • Largest Volcanoes
    • Mt. Fuji
    • Mt. Tate
    • Mt. Haku
  • Image of Sakurajima

Culture　文化

Mt. Fuji　富士山

Mt. Fuji

  • Tallest Mountain in Japan
  • Active stratovolcano
  • Symbol of the Japanese
  • Worshipped as a Kami
  • Climbing

Mt. Fuji Art

  • Cultural icon
  • Depicted in much Japanese art
  • The Great Wave off Kanagawa
    • Katsushika Hokusai

Shinto 神道

Shinto Beliefs

  • Nature Religion
    • Gods are in all things
    • Natural wonder and beauty of the landscape
  • With Buddhism, forms the basis of Japanese spirituality
